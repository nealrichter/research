## University Courses

### U of Utah — Interpretability of LLMs (Spring 2026)
[https://utah-llm-interpretability.notion.site/s26](https://utah-llm-interpretability.notion.site/s26)
*Spring 2026*

Spring 2026 course focused on interpretability methods for large language models. Requires strong understanding of transformer architecture and LLM training stages (pretraining, instruction fine-tuning, alignment).

### U of Utah — Mechanistic Interpretability Seminar
[https://utah-mechanistic-interpretability.notion.site](https://utah-mechanistic-interpretability.notion.site)
*Ongoing seminar*

A graduate seminar focused on the emerging field of mechanistic interpretability — understanding the internal workings of large language models and complex neural networks. Part of the university's broader AI track offerings.

**Instructor:** Ana Marasović

### Duke University — Explainable AI (XAI) Specialization
[https://www.coursera.org/specializations/explainable-artificial-intelligence-xai](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/explainable-artificial-intelligence-xai)
*October 2024*

A 3-course Coursera specialization covering XAI concepts, interpretable machine learning, and advanced explainability techniques for LLMs and computer vision models. Includes hands-on Python labs. Designed for learners with basic to intermediate ML background.

**Instructor:** Brinnae Bent, PhD

---

### Boston College — MT875: Mechanistic Interpretability
[https://sites.google.com/bc.edu/eli-grigsby/mt875-mechanistic-interpretability](https://sites.google.com/bc.edu/eli-grigsby/mt875-mechanistic-interpretability)
*Fall 2024*

Graduate course examining the geometric aspects of deep learning theory and mechanistic interpretability of transformers. Topics include the superposition hypothesis, feedforward ReLU networks, sequence-to-sequence architectures, and symmetries in overparameterized networks. Aimed at graduate students in mathematics and theoretical CS.

**Instructor:** Eli Grigsby

---

### Harvard University — Explainable AI: From Simple Predictors to Complex Generative Models
[https://interpretable-ml-class.github.io/](https://interpretable-ml-class.github.io/)
*Spring 2023*

Course covering the full XAI landscape: interpretable models (rule-based, prototype-based), post-hoc explanations (feature attributions, counterfactuals), mechanistic interpretability, and connections to fairness, robustness, and privacy. Includes coverage of LLMs (GPT-3) and diffusion models (DALL-E 2).

**Instructors:** Hima Lakkaraju, Ike Lage, Jiaqi Ma, Suraj Srinivas

## Self-Study & Research Guides

### Neel Nanda — A Barebones Guide to Mechanistic Interpretability Prerequisites
[https://www.neelnanda.io/mechanistic-interpretability/prereqs](https://www.neelnanda.io/mechanistic-interpretability/prereqs)
*2022 (updated ongoing)*

Practical guide covering the core math (linear algebra, probability, calculus), coding skills (Python, NumPy, PyTorch, einops/einsum), and transformer architecture knowledge needed to enter mechanistic interpretability research. Emphasizes hands-on coding over exhaustive theory.

**Authors:** Neel Nanda, Jess Smith

---

### Neel Nanda — How To Become A Mechanistic Interpretability Researcher
[https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/jP9KDyMkchuv6tHwm/how-to-become-a-mechanistic-interpretability-researcher](https://www.alignmentforum.org/posts/jP9KDyMkchuv6tHwm/how-to-become-a-mechanistic-interpretability-researcher)
*2023 (updated July 2024)*

A "learn by doing" roadmap structured in three stages: Learning the Ropes (≤1 month), Mini-projects (2–4 weeks), and Full Projects. Covers how to develop research skills, use LLMs as learning tools, seek mentorship, and navigate the evolving mech interp landscape.

**Author:** Neel Nanda

### NeurIPS 2024 — Interpretable AI: Past, Present and Future
[https://interpretable-ai-workshop.github.io/](https://interpretable-ai-workshop.github.io/)
